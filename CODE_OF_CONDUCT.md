# Contributor Code of Conduct

CSA-in-a-Box is an open reference platform built and used across federal, regulated commercial, and partner contexts. We hold contributions and conversations in this repository to a professional standard so the project remains useful and welcoming to a broad set of contributors.

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in the project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Standards we hold

Behaviors that strengthen the project:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the project and the user
- Showing empathy toward other community members
- Disclosing potential conflicts of interest (employer, customer engagement, paid-for-position) when relevant to a technical discussion

Behaviors that do not have a place here:

- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information (physical address, email, customer identity) without explicit permission
- Sharing customer-confidential information, briefings, or roadmap details a customer has shared in private
- Sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention
- Posting credentials, secrets, or proprietary data from any organization
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Customer-confidentiality clause (specific to this repo)

This project is frequently used in the context of customer engagements and competitive evaluations. Contributors must not include in any PR, issue, discussion, commit message, or comment:

- Specific customer names or any information that identifies a customer's roadmap, evaluation, or procurement state
- Verbatim quotes from briefings, meetings, or private channels
- Personal names of individuals at customer organizations
- Internal acronyms or codenames that are not public

When a contribution would benefit from real-world context, generalize the framing to a class of organizations ("a federal mission agency operating across distributed centers"; "a regulated financial institution with FedRAMP requirements") and reference only publicly disclosed strategies or open data.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for other behaviors deemed inappropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (issues, PRs, discussions, the docs site, any project-affiliated Slack / Teams channel) and also when an individual is representing the project or its community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the maintainers (see [`MAINTAINERS.md`](MAINTAINERS.md) for current contacts). All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident. Reports will be handled in confidence to the maximum extent possible.

For incidents that involve a potential breach of customer confidentiality (per the clause above), the response will additionally include:

1. Immediate triage of the offending content
2. Removal or redaction of the affected commit, issue, or PR (`git history rewrite` if necessary)
3. Notification of the affected customer if the maintainers determine notification is warranted
